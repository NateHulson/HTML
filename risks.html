1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

During the creation of a project the developers, group or company could run into a few risks that ultimately lead to the project being entirely scrapped and doomed to fail. Some of the risks that can lead a project to this form of failure are:

Scheduling

Scheduling is an important factor that needs to be taken very seriously and planned out almost perfectly otherwise parts of the project could take more time than originally is required which can set back parts of the project or even the whole project and can result in the development cost slowly creeping higher and higher and if a company is backing the project up they may choose to cancel it if it doesnâ€™t meet a certain point or functionality within a specific timeframe.

Costs

Relating back to the scheduling, the cost plays a main role with a projects development as it has to be used to pay the developers, resources and hardware for the project and even excess stuff such as QA testers and sometimes engine licenses (The license for engines is more within the game development business). The cost of the project could be raised due to scope creep (which is an increase in the projects requirements over the lifecycle) , poor scheduling/ planning or even simply poor usage of the money by the company or group.

Perfromance Risks

Performance risk is the risk that when a project is complete, it may fail to perform the way a business requires or does not perform the way it was intended by the business. Performance risks can also lead into the schedule and cost risks if any of the technical problems increases the duration of the projects lifespan which at the same time will increase the need of cost for the project.

Risks associated with external hazards

External hazards typically aren't as common but are highly unexpected, there are various types of external hazards that can ange from natural disasters, electromagntic interference, terrorism etc. Often a project should be backed up consistently incase any external hazard does take place or it'll result in the entire project being restarted from stage 1 and will need to cost extremely more as you may have to repair/ replace existing hardware.
